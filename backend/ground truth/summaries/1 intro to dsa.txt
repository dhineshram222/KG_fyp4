Data structures provide a systematic way to organize and manage data in computer memory for maximum efficiency. They transform raw data—quantities or symbols—into meaningful information by providing a logical structure. The primary goal is to optimize resource usage (space) and execution speed (time). Classification includes primitive types like integers and non-primitive types like arrays or lists. Structures are further divided into linear (sequential) and non-linear (hierarchical/random) categories. Common operations include traversing, searching, sorting, and manipulating data elements. In practice, Stacks implement undo/redo features in applications like Microsoft Word or Google Docs. Arrays serve as the foundation for storing bitmap images as a series of pixel squares. Graphs are essential for mapping social network connections and friendship relationships. Ultimately, a program is defined as a combination of algorithms and data structures working together.
