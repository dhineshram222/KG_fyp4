Carrier Sense Multiple Access (CSMA) requires stations to sense whether a channel is idle or busy before transmitting to reduce collisions. CSMA/CD (Collision Detection) monitors for interference during transmission and stops immediately if a collision is detected to save bandwidth. It utilizes a jam signal and a random back-off period before retransmitting, making it the standard for wired Ethernet (802.3). In contrast, CSMA/CA (Collision Avoidance) proactively avoids collisions by waiting for a random time if the medium is sensed as busy. This method is essential for wireless Wi-Fi (802.11) because collision detection is technically impossible in wireless environments. CSMA/CA also addresses unique challenges like the hidden terminal problem through solutions such as RTS/CTS exchanges. While CSMA/CD is effective at managing recovery after a collision occurs, CSMA/CA is effective before a collision can happen. Ultimately, CSMA/CD is optimized for local area networks, whereas CSMA/CA prioritizes minimizing collision risk to maintain high efficiency.
