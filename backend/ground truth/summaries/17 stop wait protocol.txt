The Stop-and-Wait protocol is a flow and error control mechanism used in the OSI data link and transport layers. It operates on a half-duplex/unidirectional basis, where the sender transmits one frame and stops to wait for an acknowledgment (ACK) before sending the next. This sequential process ensures accuracy and simplicity, making it easy to implement for basic communication. While efficient for local area networks (LANs) or with large packet sizes, it can be inefficient for large data volumes. Significant issues include potential infinite waiting periods if data or ACKs are lost in the network. Furthermore, delayed ACKs or data may cause the sender to retransmit unnecessarily or misidentify subsequent packets.
