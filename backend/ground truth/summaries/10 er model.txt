The Entity-Relationship (ER) model is a high-level conceptual tool that serves as a blueprint for database design, representing the overall logical structure of a system. It consists of three basic components: entities, attributes, and relationships. Entities are real-world objects, categorized as strong (independent with a primary key) or weak (dependent on strong entities). Attributes describe an entity's properties and can be key, composite, multi-valued, or derived. Relationships represent associations between entities, ranging from one-to-one to many-to-many mappings. Visually, ER diagrams use rectangles for entities, ellipses for attributes, and diamonds for relationships to depict these connections clearly. This conceptual design phase helps specify requirements and facilitates easier communication between database stakeholders before implementation.
