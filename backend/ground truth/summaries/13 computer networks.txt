A computer network consists of a group of computers connected via wires or wireless links to share data, messages, and resources. Key features include high communication speed, centralized backup, security through authentication, and hardware/software sharing. Networks are classified by size: PAN (Personal Area Network) handles personal devices within a short 10-meter range. LAN (Local Area Network) serves restricted areas like offices or schools using technologies such as Ethernet or Wi-Fi. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network) interconnects multiple LANs across a city or town, spanning 5 to 50 kilometers. WAN (Wide Area Network) covers large geographical regions like countries, with the Internet being the largest global example. Connection stability is maintained through intermediary devices such as switches, hubs, routers, and bridges. Modern trends in the field include cloud computing for on-demand resource access and BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) policies. While offering efficiency and online collaboration, networks face challenges like initial setup costs and security vulnerabilities. Ultimately, these systems enable global connectivity, allowing users to communicate and share resources regardless of distance.
