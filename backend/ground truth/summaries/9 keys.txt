Keys are essential in DBMS to uniquely identify records and maintain data consistency. A Super Key is a broad set of one or more attributes that identify a tuple, often containing extra or extraneous data. Candidate Keys are derived from super keys as the minimal set required for unique identification. From these, a Primary Key is selected; it must be unique and non-nullable to identify every row. Alternate Keys are the remaining candidate keys not chosen as the primary key. Unique Keys also ensure distinct values but, unlike primary keys, may permit null values. Composite Keys consist of a combination of two or more columns to identify a record. Foreign Keys establish relationships between tables, ensuring referential integrity by linking to a primary key in another table.
