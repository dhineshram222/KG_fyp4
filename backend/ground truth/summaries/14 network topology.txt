Network topology refers to the physical or logical arrangement of nodes used to establish communication within a computer network. Point-to-point connects two devices directly, while bus topology links all nodes to a single shared cable, offering low cost but high risk if the main cable fails. Ring topology arranges nodes in a closed loop using "token passing" for data flow, whereas star topology centers all connections around a hub or switch for better management and scalability. Mesh topology features interconnected nodes with multiple paths for data, ensuring high reliability despite increased installation costs. Tree topology utilizes a hierarchical structure combining bus and star traits, while hybrid topology integrates multiple different layouts to meet specific organizational needs. Each arrangement varies significantly in terms of cost, complexity, and fault tolerance.
